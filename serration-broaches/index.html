FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a serration broach?
A serration broach is a precision cutting tool used to machine a series of parallel ridges (serrations) inside a bore. The serrations provide a mechanical interlock between a shaft and hub, transferring torque and preventing relative rotation. Serration broaches are used for steering columns, shaft couplings, aerospace fasteners, and anti-rotation joints.
What is the difference between serrations and splines?
Splines are typically used for sliding or rotating connections and have a defined standard (DIN 5480, ASA B92.1 etc.). Serrations are generally used for press-fit, friction-locked, or anti-rotation connections and are often custom to the product design. Serration tooth profiles are usually triangular (V-form) while spline profiles are involute or straight-sided.

Yes. All serration broaches at SK Broach are manufactured to customer drawings. Please supply your drawing in DXF, PDF, or STEP format. We will review the drawing, confirm feasibility, and provide a quotation within 24 hours.
