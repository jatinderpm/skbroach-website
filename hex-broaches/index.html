Frequently Asked Questions

What is a hex broach?
A hex broach (hexagonal broach) is a precision cutting tool used to machine a hexagonal (six-sided) bore inside a workpiece. It is pushed or pulled through a pre-drilled round hole, removing material progressively until a precise hexagonal bore is formed. Hex broaches are used in the production of Allen key sockets, hex drive components, valve actuators, and torque tools.
What sizes of hex broaches do you manufacture?
SK Broach manufactures hexagonal broaches with across-flats dimensions from 4mm to 75mm in both metric (DIN/ISO) and imperial (inch) series. Non-standard sizes are also available to customer drawings.
